Responsibilities of the Citizen...

Perhaps more favored than any other waste management strategy, including recycling, is waste prevention.Electronics donated for reuse have a longer shelf life and are kept out of the waste management system for longer.But caution should be exercised when giving away such things; they must be in good functioning order.
Reuse not only benefits society but is also an environmentally preferable choice. Schools, charities, and families with limited resources can use technology that they otherwise couldn't buy by giving used devices.
E-waste should never be thrown out with regular trash or home rubbish. This ought to be separated on the premises, sold, or given to other charities.

While buying electronic products opt for those that...

  • are made with fewer toxic constituents
  • use recycled content
  • are energy efficient
  • are designed for easy upgrading or disassembly
  • utilize minimal packaging
  • offer leasing or take back options
  • have been certified by regulatory authorities.
  • Customers should opt for upgrading their computers or other electronic items to the latest versions rather than buying new equipments.
  • NGOs should adopt a participatory approach in management of e-wastes
